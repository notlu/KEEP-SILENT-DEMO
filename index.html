KEEP SILENT: SUPPRESSING HALLUCINATORY SPEECH IN END-TO-END VIDEO-TO-AUDIO GENERATION

Abstract Results Demo

Abstract

While end-to-end video-to-audio (V2A) generation has demonstrated promising results, the persistent occurrence of hallucinatory speech remains a key challenge for current V2A models. Hallucinatory speech refers to audio that exhibits the acoustic properties of human speech while containing incorrect or nonsensical linguistic content. The V2A task aims to generate reliable audio aligned with video at the sound event level. However, extensive speech content in the training data leads V2A models to produce linguistically unclear speech and generate it in inappropriate scenarios. To address this challenge, we explore three distinct data processing methods based on audio signal processing to suppress speech in the training data. Then, exploiting forgetting phenomenon during fine-tuning, we effectively mitigate hallucinatory speech generation by fine-tuning V2A model on processed data. Besides, we propose a reliable metric to assess the frequency of hallucinatory speech. Experimental results show that our approach effectively suppresses hallucinatory speech while preserving the quality of generated sound effects and music.

Main Results

We investigate the forgetting phenomenon during fine-tuning V2A models. By leveraging this phenomenon through fine-tuning on our data, we effectively reduced hallucinatory speech while maintaining the quality of generated sound effects and music.
